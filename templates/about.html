We humbly acknowledge the blessings of two revered spiritual leaders whose divine grace and guidance enabled the construction of our temple:

  • Sri Sri Ganapathi Sachidananda Swamiji — Revered pontiff of Avadootha Datta Peetham
  • Sri Sri Datta Vijayananda Theertha Swamy — Distinguished spiritual leader and successor

Our history began in 1996 when we received Swamiji's padukas as a symbol of divine blessings. The temple started on a humble 3 cents of land donated by devoted followers and has grown to nearly 100 cents today.

Now officially known as Sri Kalyana Venkateswara Swami Sametha Asta Lakshmi Devalayam, we serve devotees of Parchur and surrounding villages through daily worship, community seva, and the Datta Deeksha programme.

{% trans "Feeding the body, nourishing the soul — our most cherished community mission." %}

Friday Annadanam Seva

By the divine grace of Goddess Annapurna, we have been performing Annadanam Seva every Friday for 8+ years. A special pooja is conducted every Friday invoking Goddess Annapurna's blessings, followed by free food distribution. This service is made possible through the generous donations of our devoted community.

Datta Deeksha Initiative

For over 20 years, our devotees have participated in the Datta Deeksha programme — visiting 10+ villages and collecting a handful of rice from every household. This rice is then donated to Mysore Dattapitam for Annadanam — embodying compassion, community service, and the spirit of selfless giving.

Datta Humanity Service Team

A dedicated group of devotees embodying the spirit of compassion and service. Always ready to help, they provide assistance in various community situations — from emergency relief to temple activities. The team proudly wears a uniform dress code symbolising unity, devotion, and the teachings of Sri Ganapathi Sachidananda Swamiji.

Mathru Mandalli Team

A vital part of our temple community — devoted women who diligently maintain the temple's upkeep and participate in all religious activities. They also prepare and serve food at Annapurna Mandiram during Friday Annadanam, nourishing both body and soul with love and maternal devotion.
